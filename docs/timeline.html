Historical Time-Line of Period Products

Care for women's health

Photo by The evolution of menstrual products on mymed.com

Start

9th Century

1800s: The first commercially available disposable menstrual pads appear in the United States, made from wood pulp and cotton.

Late 19th Century

1879: Dr. A.J. Hawley patents the first menstrual cup in the United States.

Early 20th Century

1920s: Kimberly-Clark introduces the first disposable menstrual pad called "Kotex," revolutionizing menstrual product options for women.

Mid-20th Century

1930s: Tampax introduces the first commercially successful tampon with an applicator. 1940s: Disposable tampons with applicators gain popularity.

Late 20th Century

1970s: The modern menstrual cup, similar to those we know today, is developed by various companies. 1970s-1980s: Reusable cloth pads and eco-friendly menstrual product options gain popularity due to environmental concerns.

21st Century

2000s: The menstrual cup market expands, with several brands offering different designs and materials. 2010s: There is a growing focus on sustainable and eco-friendly menstrual products, leading to the rise of reusable cloth pads, period underwear, and organic disposable options.
